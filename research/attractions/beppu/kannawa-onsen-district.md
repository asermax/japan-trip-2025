## Cultural & Historical Significance

Kannawa serves as the potential birthplace of Beppu Hot Springs with documented history since 1276, functioning as the historical anchor for understanding Beppu's development through the Meiji period (when modern boring techniques created 1,000+ wells) to its current status as one of Japan's premier onsen destinations. [ðŸ”—](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beppu_Onsen)

The traditional hell-steamed cooking technique unique to Kannawa dates to the Edo period and represents a living cultural practice connecting geothermal geography with culinary heritage, showcasing sustainable use of natural resources. [ðŸ”—](https://oita-tourism.com/en/onsenStories/jigokumushi) [ðŸ”—](https://www.japan-experience.com/plan-your-trip/to-know/japanese-food/jigoku-mushi-cuisine)

As a designated Important Cultural Landscape, Kannawa represents active preservation of traditional onsen town character through the steam-rising streetscape, traditional wooden architecture, and continued use of geothermal resources for daily life (cooking, bathing, heating). [ðŸ”—](http://oitaisan.com/english/heritage/the-steam-of-kannawa-hot-springs/) [ðŸ”—](https://travel-beppu.com/en/tourism/kannawa/)
