## Basic Information

![Nakazakicho district in Osaka's Kita Ward](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c7/Kakudacho%2C_Kita_Ward%2C_Osaka%2C_Osaka_Prefecture_530-0017%2C_Japan_-_panoramio.jpg)
*Street view of Kita Ward, Osaka showing the preserved architectural character similar to Nakazakicho. Source: Wikimedia Commons*

Nakazakicho is a rare 500 square meter area in Osaka's Kita ward that miraculously survived World War II firebombings, preserving its Taisho (1912-26) and early Showa Era (1926-89) architecture. [ðŸ”—](https://voyapon.com/nakazakicho-bohemian-neighborhood-osaka/) Since around 2000, the neighborhood has transformed into Osaka's premier bohemian arts district, attracting creative types, vintage cafes, and independent galleries to its narrow alleyways. [ðŸ”—](https://zentishotels.com/en/osaka/neighborhood/1850/)

The neighborhood's wooden-framed residences and nagaya tenement houses escaped the devastating firebombing raids during World War II's final days, making it one of the few areas in Osaka to preserve its pre-war architecture. [ðŸ”—](https://zentishotels.com/en/osaka/neighborhood/1850/) The houses, built close together for structural strength, survived through the decades, and because no postwar land readjustment was carried out, the neighborhood is still crisscrossed by narrow lanes today. [ðŸ”—](https://zentishotels.com/en/osaka/neighborhood/1850/)

## Visiting Information

### Getting There

**By Subway (Most Direct):**
- Nakazakicho Station on the Tanimachi Line (Osaka Metro) - exits directly into the neighborhood [ðŸ”—](https://insideosaka.com/nakazakicho/)
- From Hotel Universal Port: Take JR to Nishikujo, transfer to Osaka Metro Chuo Line to Tanimachi-Yonchome, then Tanimachi Line to Nakazakicho (approximately 45 minutes)

**By Walking from Umeda/Osaka Station:**
- 10-15 minute walk (approximately 1km) from Umeda Station [ðŸ”—](https://insideosaka.com/nakazakicho/)
- From Hankyu Umeda Station's Chayamachiguchi exit: Exit the ticket gates, make a U-turn, descend the escalators, and turn right through a narrow passage filled with shops [ðŸ”—](https://insideosaka.com/nakazakicho/)
- Alternative: Higashi-Umeda Station (Tanimachi Line) is also nearby [ðŸ”—](https://subway.osakametro.co.jp/en/station_guide/T/t19/map.php)

**Payment:** Prepaid IC cards (Icoca, Suica, Pasmo) work on all transport [ðŸ”—](https://insideosaka.com/getting-around-osaka/)
